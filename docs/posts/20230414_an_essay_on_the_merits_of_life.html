A long and happy life is always the goal. But if you had to choose between one of them, what would it be?

That’s what I caught myself thinking while reading Ikigai(TODO add link)

  • [ ] Is happiness always simply defined?
    • Could easily be happy just spending time with my friends and family, having tea and reading books. I know I would like that, because at the end of the day, these are the things that matter most to me.
    • But at the same time, I desperately love problems. I mean, the world is truly such a wonderful place, full of so wonderful opportunities and ideas worth exploring, I feel like standing behind to watch just is not me.
    • So far: bouncing back and forth. I think this is life. Trying to explore it all, and focusing on getting the most out of everything.
    • If it costs me in the long run, I will be saddened, but should I? The philosophy of focusing around longevity is strange; focusing on getting old instead of living the moment? I mean, what’s the point in living till 150 if I am not with the ones I have loved, the ones I have grown to call friends, the ones that kept me company in this journey. If I’m not the best version of myself, will I be of any help to them? Will I actually give the world back this unbelievable chance it has given me? Again and again?
