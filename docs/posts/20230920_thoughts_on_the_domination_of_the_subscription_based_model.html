Some thoughts on the domination of the subscription based model.

I am not sure where I stand on that. On the one hand, it may allow for far greater material comfort, for a plethora of services once available only to those who had some significant degree of financial stability …

However, I’m also thinking of how sideways this may go.

At this point, a subscription means that you essentially fuel the service provider and in turn, they offer some standard degree of freedom (?) compared to the free alternatives.

I’m part of the generation that witnessed a crucial moment in computers. The shift from local computing and sitting in front of a computer solely for work, to their domination, through Internet connectivity, in almost every aspect of our lives - entertainment, news, work, communication. And so, we witnessed many free services rise, become essentially synonyms to their field and … fall to their subscription-powered TODO enemies.

To give in example around media services, playing music, YouTube and Spotify. For a really long time, should I want to listen to music, I would just launch my browser, and head to that particularly unchanged through time website, type the title of the song, maybe append “lyrics” to it as well, and drift away, to the rhythm of music. Modifying my browser to have youtube as a search engine, or creating a small script that would automate that process felt justified. At some point I think there were no ads. Maybe the whole business model was based around data collection (extremely interesting and (in a more realistic context) terrifying). Maybe they thought that through proper data management and statistical analysis, they could feed this data into their AI models, and better serve the user.

The good part did not last long. Some ads reached us. Some particularly troubling changes started being made. More adds reached us. More changes targeted towards polarization and in increasing the difficulty for the end user to properly filter the bad content. Even more adds. To a point where If I wanted to listen to a song on my phone, running iOS, I would have to listen to 2 ads, most of the time non skip-able.
