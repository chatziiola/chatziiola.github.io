## Synopsis

Here I aim to completely analyze the process of maintaining and customizing a jekyll, github-powered site, through my own experiences and mistakes so that anyone can find a plethora of tips and important stuff I couldn't find in one place but rather scattered around the internet. It will include:
 - Detailed analysis of the site files
 - The connection between them and how to modify each one in corellation with the others

Till then: have fun :P.

## Site Organization
There is one file to which you need to pay extreme attention in order to get the most out of your site:

### The _config.yml
Navigating through this file should be plain as day even for someone that doesn't normally work with html (such as me). When I saw [augustohp's website](https://augustohp.github.com) I was fascinated by its simplicity and elegance so I immediately decided that the initial slate I would build my website on would be his. From then I made quite some improvements, *at least from my point of view*, just to bring the site closer to my taste of balance and minimalism. I had to start from somewhere just to gain an understanding of how the website works ( I didn't have the slightest idea about it ) so I immediately started reading every file there to try and see the relations between each one of them. By tweaking the files I came to the first realization: **_config.yml** is exactly what its name suggest ** . Of course it's silly reading that now, you must be like *wow, he is a total idiot*, however, it didn't seem so obvious for me then. Take a moment to compare [my index file](chatziiola/chatziiola.github.io/_config.yml) with his. It is obvious that I am keeping it waaaaay more simple than he is but, in a rather weird way, I also happen (*due to ineeficient knowledge on that matter*) to probably include more stuff than necessary.

Feel free to check [my _config.yml file](./_config.yml).
